INTERVIEW WITH ABOUT UKRANIAN SOLDIER


In the war of aggression of the Russian Federation against Ukraine, many servicemen desperately sacrificed their lives, bravely fighting for the freedom of their native country and its people. Russian aggression did not pass by the family of military servicewoman Kateryna, taking the most valuable thing - the life of her husband, the father of her two children - Ukrainian soldier Bohdan Karvatko. Kateryna met her future husband, who at that time was still a cadet, in a thematic online community. The woman recalls with a smile that Bohdan was 40 minutes late for the first date, and says: "Maybe it was the happiness I was waiting for." The wife of the fallen soldier says that her husband dreamed of becoming an officer since childhood. And this was greatly facilitated by his military uncle, who became an example for Bohdan. The man was able to realize his dream, and everyone who knew Bohdan they knew him as a real officer who always kept his word.
The serviceman sincerely dreamed of making a contribution to the modernization and development of the Ukrainian army, to root out imitation of Soviet principles, and wanted new prospects for himself and for the future of the defense structure. The serviceman wanted to go to the ATO zone to defend his native land back in 2014, but Bohdan found himself at the front only in 2022 with the beginning of the full-scale invasion of the Russian Federation into Ukraine. At first, the soldier defended the Kyiv region, and later went to the East as part of a battalion tactical group.
Bohdan did not for a second leave his thoughts that he had to defend the country. After all, he understood: "If we don't do it now, our children will do it in 20, 10 years." Kateryna remembers the last conversation with her husband, in which Bohdan said only one thing: "It's just hell here." And in this hell choosing the freedom of his native country and the peaceful future of all Ukrainian children, Bohdan and several of his comrades did not retreat from their positions in order to prevent the enemy's troops from making a breakthrough, and died heroically doing their duty. With the death of her husband, extremely difficult times have come for Kateryna and her family. At first, the woman and her children did not know where their home was, where they lived, because they had to move from place to place. The life of the family was not stable, and it was very difficult for Kateryna to overcome her grief and gather her strength. But despite the painful loss of her beloved, the woman understood that life does not have a break, and she cannot put on pause either her work or, most importantly, raising her children.
"I need to think about their future, about our little family that is left, about the safety of my children," says the woman. At the same time, Kateryna perfectly understood who should also take care of herself, because her own mental state is also quite important for the well-being of the family. Later, Kateryna and her children more or less managed to cope with the difficulties and establish a stable life, but the loss of a loved one remained disappointing for the family. The woman shares: "Having lost a loved one, you realize how happy you were before and that, in principle, you don't need everything else in the world. The most important thing is the happiness of your family and close people around you."
Kateryna says that her deceased husband has awards and medals, the title of Hero of Ukraine and the Order of the Golden Star from the president. The woman aims to preserve all of Bohdan's honors, so that his children, his future grandchildren know: their father, their grandfather is a real hero of his country, a hero who fought for their peaceful future in a free Ukraine.
During his lifetime, Bohdan dreamed that his children would get an education and develop. Kateryna supported this desire then and shares it now. You and I can realize this dream by making a donation to Kateryna's family in honor of Bohdan Karvatko - a real officer and Hero of Ukraine.
